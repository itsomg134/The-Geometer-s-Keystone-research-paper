#  The Geometer's Keystone

### *A Historical Evolution of Triangle Geometry Theory*

[![License: MIT](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-yellow.svg)](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)
[![Research](https://img.shields.io/badge/Research-History%20of%20Math-blue)]()

A comprehensive research paper tracing the 4,000-year journey of triangle geometry—from ancient Egyptian land surveying to modern computational geometry and the Encyclopedia of Triangle Centers.

---

##  Overview

The triangle is the simplest polygon, yet it has served as the fundamental building block of geometry, engineering, and mathematical thought for millennia. This repository contains a complete historical research paper that explores how our understanding of triangles evolved across civilizations and intellectual paradigms.

The paper argues that the history of triangle geometry is not merely a chronicle of accumulating theorems, but a reflection of shifting mathematical frameworks—from empirical rules to axiomatic systems, from synthetic reasoning to analytic algebra, and from isolated discoveries to unified structural theories.

##  Key Theorems & Concepts Covered

- **Euclid's *Elements*** – Congruence (SAS, ASA, SSS) and the 180° angle sum theorem
- **Law of Sines** – $\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$
- **Euler Line** – Collinearity of circumcenter, centroid, and orthocenter with $OG:GH = 1:2$
- **Feuerbach's Theorem** – The nine-point circle is tangent to the incircle and excircles
- **Ceva's Theorem** – Concurrency condition: $\frac{AF}{FB} \cdot \frac{BD}{DC} \cdot \frac{CE}{EA} = 1$
- **Napoleon's Theorem** – Equilateral triangles on any triangle form an equilateral triangle
- **Kimberling's ETC** – Over 50,000 triangle centers indexed by trilinear coordinates
